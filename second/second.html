WHO IS Shekhar

Naik was born as the son of a farmer into a nomadic Banjara family in Arekere, Karnataka. He was born blind, and his mother and 15 other members of his family were all visually impaired. When Naik was seven years old, he fell along the bank of a river and sustained a brain injury. He was brought to a nearby health camp, where doctors discovered the possibility of regaining his right eye's sight during therapy. Following surgery in Bangalore, he was able to restore 60 percent of his vision in his right eye.Soon after, his father died, and he was taken to Shimoga's Shri Sharada Devi School for the Blind. While at school, he learned to play cricket. During his summer vacations, he toiled in the fields to support his cricketing goals. His mother died when he was twelve years old. When he's not playing, he works as a sports coordinator for Samarthanam, an NGO that supports India's Cricket Association for the Blind. He is the father of two daughters.

career

After collecting 136 runs in 46 balls, he was chosen for the Karnataka team in 2000. In 2002, he was called up to the India national blind cricket team, and in 2010, he was named captain. Each side has four B1 players (who are entirely blind), three B2 players (who are half blind), and four B3 players (partially sighted). Naik is a member of the B2 team. In the 2006 World Cup, he was named Player of the Tournament. He helped the side win the inaugural T20 world cup in 2012 by scoring 134 runs in 58 balls against England in the finals. In 2014, he also led the side to victory in the Cricket World Cup, which was hosted in South Africa.He became the first blind cricketer to be awarded the Padma Shri in 2017.

Short overview of Shekhar naik

Shekhar Naik (born 7 April 1986) is a former captain of the Indian national blind cricket team and an Indian blind cricketer. In 2012 and 2014, he led India to triumphs in the T20 Blind Cricket World Cup and the Blind Cricket World Cup. The Indian government bestowed the Padma Shri, the country's fourth highest civilian honor, on Naik in 2017. He is a wicket-keeper and a right-handed batsman
