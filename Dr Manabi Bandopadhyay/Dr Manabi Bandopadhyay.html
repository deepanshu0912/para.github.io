Tiffany Brar is an Indian community volunteer

Brar is the founder of the Jyothirgamaya Foundation

She went blind as a child owing to oxygen toxicity. Brar is the founder of the Jyothirgamaya Foundation, a non-profit that educates blind individuals of all ages life skills. She is a trainer, a disability awareness campaigner, and an advocate for a more inclusive society.

 

General T. P. S. Brar and Leslie Brar have only one child, Tiffany Brar. Tiffany was born in Chennai, India, and raised in Punjab, where her father was stationed.Tiffany Brar was born with Terry Syndrome and was blind six months later.Brar traveled a lot because of her father's job. Because she was blind, she valued verbal communication and became multilingual. Brar learned to speak five Indian languages fluently during her childhood. While her father was stationed in the United Kingdom, she began her education there. Brar attended schools for the blind, integrated schools, and military schools after her family returned to India. Tiffany's father was relocated to Darjeeling after she completed her elementary school in Kerala, where she studied at the Mary Scott Home for the Blind. Brar struggled at school since he was blind. She was forced to sit in the back of the class and was occasionally denied the opportunity to respond to questions. Her Braille notes were either late or never came at all. Her parents provided her with refuge and protection.Brar was isolated and segregated by other students at her schools, and she was excluded from festivities. In 12th grade, she took first place at the CBSE board test in a school with sighted students.Brar enrolled in a bachelor's degree programme in English literature at Government College for Women, Thiruvananthapuram, which is connected with the University of Kerala, after graduating from high school in 2006. Brar began working as a telephone operator for Braille Without Borders after graduating in 2009. She visited numerous organizations and saw many impoverished blind people who were confined to their homes and without sufficient training. During one journey, she discovered that roads are slippery or covered in stones, making them unsuitable for blind people, and that in some parts of India, unsupportive societies confine blind people to their houses. This inspired her to establish the Jyothirgamaya Foundation to help blind and partially sighted youngsters with their education. Visually challenged children in Kerala are taught Malayalam, the regional language, using text-recognition software, but they are not enrolled in special schools and are not taught Braille or English. Brar organized a movement to reform the system in Jyothirgamaya. Brar earned her B.Ed in special education (visual impairment) from Sree Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda University in Coimbatore after working for two years. She started the Jyothirgamaya (meaning "leading to light") mobile school for the blind in July 2012. N. Krishnaswamy, a former police officer from Tamil Nadu, came up with the idea for the organization to support children who are disadvantaged by poverty, disability, or distance. Brar was honored by the Indian government in 2018 for her actions amid flooding in Wayanad, where she collected supplies for relief camps.Brar now travels alone around India and internationally.

 

 

Tiffany Brar began her career as a receptionist before pursuing a B.Ed in special education at Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda University. In July 2012, she founded the Jyothirgamaya Foundation to help blind and partially sighted individuals in Kerala. "If the blind cannot go to school, the school shall come to them," Brar said on establishing a mobile school for blind students. After her leadership training at Vellayani with a German lady, Sabriye Tenberken, and her Dutch partner, Paul Kronenberg, she was inspired with this notion. Brar teaches blind people Braille, mobility, basic computer skills, and life skills through the Jyothirgamaya Foundation. She has also recently launched the Road to Independence project, which organizes training camps throughout Kerala.

 

When She is an artist, teacher, and motivational speaker who represented the WWF in India during the 2016 Earth Hour campaign. She was the first Indian to earn the Holman Prize from LightHouse for the Blind and Visually Impaired in the United States in 2020. Brar launched a preparatory school and kindergarten in Thiruvananthapuram for visually-challenged children in 2019, which was inaugurated by Kerala Health Minister KK Shailaja. Brar created the Tiffy template, which was created by inventor Paul D'Souza. It is a simple template that identifies Indian cash notes in order to protect blind individuals from being defrauded by traders.
