Indian actor in the Kannada film industry T.N. Balkrishna

Tirumakudalu Narasipura Balakrishna, often known as T. N. Balakrishna or Balanna, was an Indian actor who worked in the Kannada film industry from 1916 until 1995. He was supposed to have a hearing difficulty, and some even said he was deaf. He would, however, notice the artists' lip movements and recount the dialogues on the spot. He was known for his comedic and villainous parts in films like as Muriyada Mane (1964), Bangaarada Manushya (1972), Gandhada Gudi (1973), and Kaamana Billu (1983), and he appeared in over a hundred Rajkumar films. Balakrishna is noted for playing the most parts in Kannada cinema, with over 560 appearances as heroes, villains, comedians, good Samaritans, loving fathers, and lunatics. Sudha Chandran is his ex-daughter-in-law.

 

Balakrishna was born on November 2, 1916, in Arsikere, Kingdom of Mysore, to a poor family. His mother sold him to a couple for $8 when he was four years old in order to pay for treatment for her ailing husband. After being mistreated by his adoptive parents, he fled away. Balakrishna developed lung cancer in his latter years and died of it on July 18, 1995, at the age of 78. His last film appearance was in Yama Kinkara. Here is rare interview of T.N. Balkrishna:-

 

 

In 1929, Balakrishna made his stage debut in Shri Rama Pattabhishekam. After that, he worked for a theatrical company as a stage background painter before becoming a professional sign painter.Later, for a pittance, he worked as a ticket collector for a theatrical group. With Lakshmasana Drama Company and afterwards with Gowrishankar Nataka Mandali, he returned to acting in theater. Following that, he joined Gubbi Veeranna's playgroup, a well-known theater director at the time.B. R. Panthulu, a film director who happened to be visiting the theater company, observed his performance on stage and offered him a role in a film, launching his career as a movie actor. In the film Kalachakra, he played an adversary, and his performance was praised.He performed in over a hundred Kannada films with Rajkumar, primarily in humorous roles.He founded the studio with all of his money and property in order to make the Kannada film industry self-sufficient and save money for the producers.

 

When In 1963, he created the Abhiman studio on a 20-acre tract in Kengeri, Bangalore, but it was not without its challenges.When there were no Kannada films being created, he teamed up with other performers such as Rajkumar and G. V. Iyer to make Ranadheera Kanteerava. He even gathered funds from the general public at a rate of one rupee per person to construct the studio. Balanna died in poverty as a result of the studio's failure.Television serials are still being shot at the studio today which is being looked after by Balakrishna's son, B. Ganesh and grandson Karthik.

 

He won The Best Supporting Actor award in Karnataka State Film Awards
